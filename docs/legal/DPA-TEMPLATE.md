# Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

**Template for Enterprise Customers**

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the agreement between Customer and ShipGate ("Processor") for the provision of the ShipGate service. It applies where Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of Customer under the Agreement and where required by applicable data protection law (e.g., GDPR Article 28).

---

## 1. Definitions

- **Personal Data** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual processed by Processor on behalf of Customer in connection with the Service.
- **Processing** has the meaning given in applicable data protection law.
- **Subprocessor** means any third party engaged by Processor to process Personal Data.
- **Data Subject** means the identified or identifiable individual to whom Personal Data relates.

---

## 2. Roles and Scope

Customer acts as data controller. Processor acts as data processor. This DPA applies to Processing of Personal Data necessary for the provision of the ShipGate Service (e.g., account information, OAuth-derived identifiers, audit logs, integration metadata). Processor does not receive or process Customer's source code.

---

## 3. Processor Obligations

Processor shall:

1. **Process only on documented instructions** — Process Personal Data only in accordance with Customer's documented instructions and this DPA, unless required by law.
2. **Confidentiality** — Ensure persons authorised to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations.
3. **Security** — Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect Personal Data, including:
   - Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3)
   - Encryption at rest for sensitive credentials (AES-256-GCM)
   - Access controls (RBAC, least privilege)
   - Audit logging
4. **Subprocessors** — Maintain a list of Subprocessors; notify Customer of additions; ensure Subprocessors are bound by obligations no less protective than this DPA. Current Subprocessors: Stripe, GitHub, Google, Slack, Vercel, Railway (as applicable to Customer's use).
5. **Assistance** — Assist Customer in responding to Data Subject requests and in ensuring compliance with Customer's obligations under applicable data protection law, where relevant.
6. **Deletion/Return** — Upon termination, delete or return Personal Data as instructed by Customer, unless retention is required by law.

---

## 4. Customer Obligations

Customer shall:
- Provide lawful instructions for Processing.
- Ensure it has a lawful basis for Processing and appropriate notices/consents where required.
- Use the Service in accordance with applicable law.

---

## 5. Subprocessors

Processor may engage Subprocessors. The current Subprocessor list and their purposes are published at [Security & Compliance](/security) (or equivalent URL). Processor will provide notice of material changes to Subprocessors. Customer may object on reasonable grounds; Processor will work in good faith to address objections.

---

## 6. International Transfers

Where Personal Data is transferred outside the EEA/UK, Processor will use adequacy decisions, Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), or other approved transfer mechanisms as required by applicable law.

---

## 7. Audits and Inspections

Processor will make available information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA. Customer may request an audit; Processor will cooperate and may provide certifications or summary reports instead of full audit access where commercially reasonable.

---

## 8. Liability

Liability under this DPA is subject to the limitation of liability in the Agreement. Nothing in this DPA excludes or limits liability that cannot be excluded or limited under applicable law.

---

## 9. Term and Termination

This DPA survives termination of the Agreement for as long as Processor retains Personal Data. Upon termination, Processor will delete or return Personal Data as instructed within 30 days (or such shorter period as agreed), except where retention is required by law.

---

## 10. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the law specified in the Agreement. Where the GDPR applies, the supervisory authority is determined by Customer's establishment or representative.
